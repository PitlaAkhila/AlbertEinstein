Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, into a secular Ashkenazi Jewish family in Ulm, Kingdom of WÃ¼rttemberg, German Empire. His parents were Pauline Koch and Hermann Einstein, an engineer and merchant. The family relocated to Munich around 1880. The Elektrotechnische Fabrik J. Einstein & Cie, a business that produced direct current electrical equipment, was founded by Einstein's father and his uncle Jakob. From a young age, Einstein was a math and physics prodigy, rising to a level of proficiency years ahead of his contemporaries. During the course of one summer, 12-year-old Einstein taught himself algebra and Euclidean geometry.

He was hired as a lecturer at the University of Bern in 1908 after gaining notoriety as a distinguished scientist.As he delivered a lecture on electrodynamics and the relativity principle at the University of Zurich the next year, Alfred Kleiner suggested he be considered for a newly created professorship in theoretical physics by the faculty. In 1909, Einstein was made an associate professor.

EXPERIENCES

  • Clerk, Swiss Patent Office, Bern, 1902-1909.
  • Lecturer, University of Bern, 1908.
  • Associate professor of physics, University of Zurich, 1909-1910.
  • Professor of physics, Unversity of Prague, 1911.
  • Professor of physics, Swiss Federal Polytechnic Institute, 1912-1913.
  • Director, Kaiser Wilhelm Physical Institution, Berlin, 1914-1933.
  • Professor of Theoretical Physics, Institute for Advanced Study, Princeton, 1933-1955.

    • HOBBIES

    Sailing, reading, and playing the violin and piano were among Albert Einstein's pastimes. Also, he kept travel diaries, which are currently housed in the Jerusalem-based Albert Einstein Archives.

    Over his life, Einstein owned multiple sailboats. He kept one in Caputh, Germany, which the Nazis confiscated along with his cottage in 1933. He once owned a yacht by the name of Tinnef while residing in the United States.
